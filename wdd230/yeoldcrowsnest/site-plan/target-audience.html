Description

The majority of participants who will use this site fall into one of two categories.

The first group, defined as reenactors, are mostly hobbiests. They fall into three categories:

  • Farbs - place emphasis on the overall experience rather than authenticity.
  • Mainstream - make an effort to appear authentic but may appear out of character when an audience is not present.
  • Progressives - value research and seek an immersive experience that is a close to the original experience as possible.

Overall, reenactors are interested in getting their informaiton out to the community. They want to inform the public of upcoming events and shows. Reenactors enjoy educating others and may seek employment opportunities in period films and documentaries.

The second group of users includes those who wish to learn more about the history of piracy. These individuals are interested in experiencing past narratives in an immersive way. They may be researching the topic for school or work or simple to learn more about this subject.

Personas

  • Cameron is a 28 year old college student working on his final project in film school. He works part time as a waiter to support his education and with any free time that he has, Cameron likes to watch classic black and white movies. His current project is a swashbuckling epic and has been made with mostly friends and family. To complete his project, Cameron has written a climactic fight scene involving over 100 actors. He now needs a website to find them.

  • Becka is a 32 year old mother of six children. She has next to no free time but gets support from her family to indulge her passion for live history; specifically pirate history. The group Becka joined is in need of information to help them prepare for an upcoming event. Becka needs to know what the weather will be like in order to inform her crew as to how they should dress.

Scenarios

  • Cameron is making a video for his film class. He is on a shoestring budget but needs a motley band of pirates to film the final sequence. He decides the best way to get the shots he needs and stay under budget is to hire extras that can bring their own propts and costumes. Cameron looks over the different groups on Ye Old Crow's Nest and messages several to schedule some one-on-one meetings to discuss his specific requirements.

  • Madison wants to learn more about the golden age of piracy. Although she has gone to wikipedia and several other sites for information; yet what she really wants is to experience the life of a pirate. Checking the events section, Madison learns there is a festival "Treasure Island 2021" scheduled to take place in Fish Haven, Idaho. Madison signs up for more information and happily marks her calendar.

  • KJ and his friends just got back from a trip to Soda Springs, Idaho. Unfortunately during all the activity, KJ lost his camera. Luckily, KJ made some friends on the trip who posted their pictures to the gallery on this site. KJ is able to recover many of of the memories he and his friends made and share them with his friends and family at home.

  • Becka's reenactment troupe is scheduled to perform in Preston, Idaho later today. Tasked with letting her group know what the weather will be like in order for the team to dress appropriately, Becka checks the weather forecast on the site and sees it is mostly cloudy with a 24% chance of rain. Her group won't need to bring sunscreen. Instead, Becka messages everyone and suggests they bring an umbrella.

  • Amanda is an aspiring writer. She wants to build her portfolio and decides to write several short biographies on the lives of several known pirates. Her work is well recieved by the Crow's Nest community and along with some historical correction Amanda gains the confidence to begin work on a fictictious novel entitled, "RoseBee McPegLeg" about the misadventures of a good natured yet traviesa heroine pirate.
